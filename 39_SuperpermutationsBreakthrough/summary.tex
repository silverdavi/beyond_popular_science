A significant combinatorial breakthrough from an unlikely source: an anonymous 4chan post (2011) responding to a question about anime episode viewing orders. Superpermutations are strings containing every possible ordering of n symbols as substrings. For years, mathematicians believed the minimal length followed the pattern of factorial sums observed in small cases. The anonymous poster derived a rigorous lower bound, modelling the problem as path optimisation through a permutation graph. This proof remained obscure until 2014 when mathematician Robin Houston rediscovered it, leading to the disproof of the long-standing conjecture and establishing new bounds on this combinatorial problem—with the original derivation still officially credited to \QSOpen{}Anonymous 4chan Poster.\QSClose{}
