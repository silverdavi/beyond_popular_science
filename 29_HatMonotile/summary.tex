The \QSOpen{}Einstein problem\QSClose{}, named from German \textit{\QSOpen{}ein Stein\QSClose{}} (one stone), asks whether a monotile could tile the plane only aperiodically. In 2022, David Smith, a retired printer experimenting with shapes, discovered the thirteen-sided \QSOpen{}hat\QSClose{} that solved this sixty-year puzzle. The hat tiles infinitely without ever repeating its pattern. The study of tilings previously revealed a connection between mathematics and physical quasicrystals, a discovery that won a Nobel Prize in 2011.
